How Many Calories Should You Eat on Average?

Understanding your daily calorie requirements is essential for maintaining a healthy lifestyle. Your intake depends on factors such as age, activity level, and health conditions. Below, we break down the average calorie needs for **females, males, and children**.

Maintaining a balanced diet with **proteins, healthy fats, and carbohydrates** helps meet your body's energy needs.

Females

AgeCalories
19–30 years2,000–2,400
31–60 years1,600–2,200
61+ years1,600–2,000

Pregnant or nursing women need **significantly more calories**.

Males

AgeCalories
19–30 years2,400–3,000
31–60 years2,200–3,000
61+ years2,000–2,600

Highly active individuals may require **extra calories**.

Children

AgeCalories
2–4 yearsM: 1,000–1,600 | F: 1,000–1,400
5–8 yearsM: 1,200–2,000 | F: 1,200–1,800
9–13 yearsM: 1,600–2,600 | F: 1,400–2,200
14–18 yearsM: 2,000–3,200 | F: 1,800–2,400

Growing children need **a nutrient-dense diet**.
